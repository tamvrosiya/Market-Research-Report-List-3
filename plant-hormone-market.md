<p><p>Plant hormones play a crucial role in the growth and development of plants. The five main types of plant hormones are abscisic acid, auxins, cytokinins, ethylene, and gibberellins. Abscisic acid regulates seed dormancy and stress responses. Auxins control cell growth and development. Cytokinins promote cell division and delay aging. Ethylene regulates fruit ripening and senescence. Gibberellins stimulate stem elongation and seed germination. The market for plant hormones is driven by their importance in agriculture and horticulture for enhancing crop yield and quality.</p></p>
