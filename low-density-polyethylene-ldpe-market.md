<p><p>Low-Density Polyethylene (LDPE) is a type of polymer produced through different processes, namely Autoclave Process and Tubular Process. The Autoclave Process involves high pressure and temperature to create LDPE with variable properties suitable for film production, while the Tubular Process uses continuous extrusion through a tubular reactor to produce LDPE with more consistent properties for applications like bags and bottles. Both market types cater to different industries and end-uses based on the manufacturing process and resulting product characteristics.</p></p>
