<p><p>Non-absorbable surgical sutures are categorized into various types based on the material used. Polypropylene sutures are commonly used for tissue approximation in surgeries due to their strength and flexibility. Nylon sutures are known for their minimal tissue reaction and ease of handling. Stainless steel sutures are strong and resistant to corrosion, making them suitable for use in orthopedic and cardiovascular surgeries. Other non-absorbable sutures include silk, polyester, and ePTFE, each with their own unique properties and applications in surgical procedures.</p></p>
