<p><p>Radon gas testing services come in two main types: passive and active. Passive testing involves the use of devices that do not require power to operate, such as charcoal canisters or alpha track detectors. These devices are typically left in the home for a designated period of time to collect data on radon levels. In contrast, active testing involves the use of electronic devices that continuously monitor radon levels and provide real-time data. Both types of testing are essential for ensuring the safety of indoor air quality.</p></p>
