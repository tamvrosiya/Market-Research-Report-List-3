<p><p>Non-starch polysaccharides enzymes are classified into two main types: soluble and insoluble. Soluble enzymes are capable of breaking down polysaccharides that are easily dissolved in water, such as gums and pectins. These enzymes are important for improving digestion and nutrient absorption. On the other hand, insoluble enzymes work on polysaccharides that are not easily dissolved in water, such as cellulose and hemicellulose. They play a crucial role in promoting gut health and regular bowel movements.</p></p>
