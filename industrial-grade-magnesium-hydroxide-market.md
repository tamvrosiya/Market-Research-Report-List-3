<p><p>Industrial grade magnesium hydroxide is produced through two main methods - chemical synthesis and physical methods. Chemical synthesis involves reacting magnesium salt with a base to yield magnesium hydroxide. On the other hand, physical methods involve precipitation or hydrothermal synthesis to obtain magnesium hydroxide. Both methods have their own advantages and disadvantages, with chemical synthesis being more commonly used due to its cost-effectiveness and scalability, while physical methods offer higher purity and control over particle size.</p></p>
