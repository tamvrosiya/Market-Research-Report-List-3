<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Distributed Fibre Optic Sensors Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Rayleigh Scattering Based Distributed Sensor</li><li>Brillouin Scattering Based Sensor</li><li>Raman Scattering Based Sensor</li><li>Interferometric Distributed Optical-Fibre Sensor</li><li>Distributed Fibre Bragg Grating Sensor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Distributed Fibre Optic Sensors utilize different mechanisms for measuring various physical parameters along the length of the fibre optic cable. Rayleigh Scattering Based Sensors use the scattering of light to detect changes in the fibre optic cable. Brillouin Scattering Based Sensors measure temperature and strain by analyzing the frequency shift of the scattered light. Raman Scattering Based Sensors measure temperature and strain based on the intensity of the scattered light. Interferometric Distributed Optical-Fibre Sensors use interference patterns to measure changes along the fibre optic cable. Distributed Fibre Bragg Grating Sensors utilize the reflection of light from periodic gratings within the cable to measure strain and temperature.</p></p>
