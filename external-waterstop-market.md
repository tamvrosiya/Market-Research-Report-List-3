<p><p>External waterstops are commonly divided into three main types: rubber, PVC, and other materials. Rubber waterstops are known for their flexibility, allowing them to adjust to movement and settlement in concrete structures. PVC waterstops are preferred for their high chemical resistance and durability against harsh environmental conditions. Other market types include materials such as hydrophilic waterstops, which swell when in contact with water to create a watertight seal. Each type offers specific advantages and is chosen based on the project's requirements.</p></p>
