<p><p>Ceramic binders can be categorized into two main types: organic and inorganic. Organic binders are made from carbon-based compounds and are typically used for applications where flexibility and chemical resistance are important. Inorganic binders, on the other hand, are made from non-carbon-based compounds and are known for their high temperature resistance and mechanical strength. Both types of binders play a crucial role in the manufacturing of ceramic products, offering different properties and advantages based on the specific application requirements.</p></p>
