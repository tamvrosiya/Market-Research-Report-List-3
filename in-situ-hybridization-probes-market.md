<p><p>In situ hybridization probes are used to detect the presence of specific nucleic acid sequences within a biological sample. There are two main types of probes used in this process: labeled DNA oligonucleotides and labeled RNA oligonucleotides. Labeled DNA oligonucleotides are short, single-stranded DNA molecules that are labeled with a fluorescent or radioactive tag, while labeled RNA oligonucleotides are similar molecules made of RNA. These probes hybridize with complementary sequences in the sample, allowing researchers to visualize and study specific genetic information.</p></p>
