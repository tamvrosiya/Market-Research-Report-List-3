<p><p>Legumes-sourced dietary fibers are classified into two main types: soluble dietary fiber and insoluble dietary fiber. Soluble dietary fiber dissolves in water and forms a gel-like substance in the digestive tract, helping to lower cholesterol and stabilize blood sugar levels. Insoluble dietary fiber does not dissolve in water and adds bulk to the stool, promoting regular bowel movements and preventing constipation. Both types of dietary fiber found in legumes help to support digestive health and overall well-being.</p></p>
