<p><p>All-vegetable shortening market can be divided into Non-Emulsion Type and Emulsion Type. Non-Emulsion Type shortening is made by simply blending solid fats like palm oil and canola oil without the use of any emulsifiers. On the other hand, Emulsion Type shortening involves the use of emulsifiers to create a stable mixture of water and oil. Non-Emulsion Type shortening is preferred for applications where a flaky texture is desired, while Emulsion Type shortening is commonly used in baked goods for a softer texture.</p></p>
