<p><p>A fixed rainfall simulator is a stationary device typically found in laboratories or research facilities. It is designed for consistent and controlled testing of soil erosion and runoff. On the other hand, a portable rainfall simulator is a compact and mobile device that can be easily transported to different field locations for on-site testing. These simulators are often used for agricultural research, environmental assessments, and soil management studies. Both types of simulators serve the purpose of replicating natural rainfall conditions for experimental purposes.</p></p>
